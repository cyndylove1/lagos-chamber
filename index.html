Our Quality Policy

The Lagos Chamber of Commerce and Industry (LCCI) is committed to consistently promoting a strong business environment and delivering value to its stakeholders.

The LCCI is committed to the implementation of a Quality Management System (QMS) in accordance with the requirements of ISO 9001:2015 and ensures compliance with relevant statutory requirements.

To effectively achieve this policy, the Chamber focuses on the following:

  • Full implementation of the Quality Management System.
  • Provision of adequate resources for effective QMS.
  • Periodic QMS audits and continuous improvement.
  • Regular review of the Quality Policy.
  • Deployment of effective technologies and processes.
  • Maintenance of documented QMS in line with ISO 9001:2015.

Services of the LCCI

The Lagos Chamber of Commerce and Industry was established for the promotion and protection of Trade and Industry and to represent and express the opinion of the business community on matters affecting trade and industry in the city of Lagos, the Lagos Port Complex, Lagos State and where applicable, the whole Federation. It is a non-profit making organisation and its income and property are applied solely for the promotion of its objectives.

It is the central organisation for collecting distributing and disseminating information of a business nature, especially regulations, tariffs, opportunities for trade and other matters affecting Home and Overseas markets.

In the pursuit of its objectives, the Chamber is committed to no political creed.

The Council of the Chamber is the highest policy making body and usually meets bi-monthly on the first Wednesday to deliberate on issues of importance to the Chamber, the business sector and the economy. Members, who are not individual members of the Council, are welcome to attend as observers.

Standing Committees and Sector Groups were setup to cater for a variety of interests.

Letters of introduction can be issued to members for business and trade purposes. The Chamber also facilitates networking with institutions abroad and confirms references of local members.

Numerous directories, trade journals and periodicals from virtually every country in the world are available to members who may have access to such publications in the Chamber’s Library and Business Information Centre.

Certificate of Origin are endorsed at a modest cost to members.

Members receive the Chamber’s journals, Economy and Business and the “Nigeria Business Directory” Annually, members receive free the Chamber’s report for the year and up-to-date list of members.The Chamber’s publications are among the leading works of reference used by overseas business in connection with trade and investment opportunities in Nigeria.

The Chamber is recognized as the official body through which local opinion on commercial and industrial matters can be presented directly to Government or other authorities both national and local. It is frequently consulted by government in advance of the preparation of new legislation affecting the business community.

Ethics & Corporate Governance

At the Lagos Chamber of Commerce and Industry (LCCI), we firmly believe that a strong ethical compass and robust corporate governance practices are the bedrock of sustainable business success and a thriving economy. We are dedicated to fostering a culture of integrity, transparency, and accountability within the Nigerian business landscape.

Our Commitment to Ethical Business Practices and Good Governance:

We understand that businesses operating with high ethical standards and well-defined governance frameworks are not only more resilient and attractive to investment but also contribute positively to the communities they serve. Through our various initiatives, we aim to:

  • Champion Ethical Awareness: Elevate the understanding and importance of ethical conduct in all business dealings.
  • Empower Through Knowledge: Provide businesses with the resources, training, and insights needed to implement and strengthen their corporate governance structures.
  • Facilitate Best Practice Sharing: Create platforms for dialogue and the exchange of knowledge on leading governance principles and practices.
  • Advocate for a Principled Ecosystem: Support policies and regulations that promote ethical behavior and sound governance across all sectors.
